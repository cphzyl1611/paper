As shown in ~\autoref{tab:log_parsing_methods}, frequency-based parsing approaches refer to methods that focus on identifying frequent string patterns in log messages and clustering similar logs based on these patterns.
Zhu et al.~\cite{zhu2019toolsbenchmarksautomatedlog} highlighted automated log parsing's importance.
He et al.~\cite{he2017drain} introduced \textit{Drain}, an online log parsing approach designed for streaming and timely log processing, essential for real-time log analysis in web service management.
Makanju et al.~\cite{makanju2009clustering} split different parts of the log through multiple iterations and built templates based on frequency of occurrence.
Mizutani et al.~\cite{mizutani2013incremental} use frequency and incremental algorithms to parse large-scale log data and build log templates in real time.
Vaarandi et al.~\cite{vaarandi2003data} based on frequent pattern mining, common pattern building templates in logs are extracted.
Frequency-based methods make it easier to identify high-frequency patterns, while low-frequency but critical anomaly patterns are easily overlooked. When the log format is complex or changes frequently, the fixed frequency analysis is difficult to adapt to diversified formats, and the analysis accuracy decreases.


\subsection{Similarity-based Parsing}
Similarity-based parsing refers to methods that group and parse logs by assessing the similarity between their pattern sequences, often using distance metrics or sequence alignment techniques to identify common structures.
Du et al.~\cite{du2018spell} introduced \textit{Spell}, an online streaming method that uses the longest common subsequence-based approach for system event logs.
Chu et al.~\cite{chu2021prefix} presented \textit{Prefix-Graph}, which combines prefix trees and probabilistic graphs for efficient parsing.
Tang et al.~\cite{tang2011logsig} based on the editing distance between log entries, the log template is generated by similarity clustering.
Messaoudi et al.~\cite{messaoudi2018search} generate templates and improve parsing accuracy by minimizing differences in log entries.
Hamooni et al.~\cite{hamooni2016logmine} use grouping and similarity matching to extract common patterns from large-scale logs.
Vega et al.~\cite{vega2015humanitarian} clustering based on the similarity between log entries.
Lin et al.~\cite{lin2016log} automatically generate log templates based on similarity matching and clustering algorithms.
However, similarity-based methods often require manual adjustment of the similarity threshold, and standardizing similarity metrics across diverse scenarios is challenging, limiting their ability to generalize effectively.  Additionally, the computation of similarity between log entries is resource-intensive, leading to inefficiencies when processing large-scale log datasets.



\subsection{Heuristics-based Parsing}
Heuristic-based parsing relies on predefined rules or heuristics to extract structured data from raw logs, using domain-specific knowledge to identify log templates and patterns.
Liu et al.~\cite{liu2022uniparser} introduced \textit{UniParser}, a unified log parser designed to capture common logging behaviors across diverse log formats, emphasizing efficient parsing.
Xiao et al. ~\cite{xiao2020lpv} proposed \textit{LPV}, a vectorization-based log parser that excels in offline and online parsing tasks.
Dai et al.~\cite{dai2020logram} introduced \textit{Logram}, which uses n-gram dictionaries for fast parsing but may lack the depth needed for capturing semantic relationships.
Jiang et al.~\cite{jiang2008automated} generate structured output based on fixed regular expressions and pattern matching.
Yu et al.~\cite{10109145} create initial groups based on the longest common pattern among log entries. It then utilizes a bidirectional tree to hierarchically add constant words to the longest common pattern, efficiently constructing the complete log template.
However, heuristic-based methods are typically manually crafted, making them less adaptable to dynamic and diverse log formats, and their scalability is limited.

\subsection{Semantic-based Parsing}
Semantic-based parsing leverages techniques such as machine learning and NLP algorithms to learn deep semantic features of log entries, enabling the extraction of log templates based on their semantic content rather than relying solely on structural patterns.
Le et al.~\cite{le2023logparsingpromptbasedfewshot} proposed \textit{LogPPT}, a prompt-based few-shot method that captures template patterns but requires significant optimization and fine-tuning, leading to slower parsing speeds.
Ma et al.~\cite{Ma_2024} explored LLM in \textit{LLMParser}, offering high accuracy at the cost of resource-intensive training.
Self-supervised approaches \textit{NuLog}~\cite{nedelkoski2020selfsupervisedlogparsing} reduce the need for labeled data but may lack precision compared to supervised methods.
\textit{DivLog}~\cite{10.1145/3597503.3639155} uses statistical divergence to identify diverse log templates but requires extensive parameter tuning, making it resource-intensive.
However, semantic-based methods tend to be structurally complex, challenging to tune, and require extensive hyperparameter adjustments and model optimization, resulting in relatively high computational costs for training and inference.

NLP enables machines to understand, interpret, and generate natural language in a way that is both meaningful and useful.
Text similarity in NLP has been a crucial area of research in recent years~\cite{zhang2022semanticsimilaritycomputingmodel, han2021semantic}. Various approaches have been proposed to enhance text embeddings and improve text mining and analysis tasks~\cite{r2020metaembeddingsnaturallanguageinference, NEURIPS2019_043ab21f}. Niu et al.~\cite{niu2021short} introduced a short text similarity computation method based on feature expansion and Siamese neural network, addressing the widely studied problem of text similarity computation in NLP. Moreover, Xu et al.~\cite{xu2024reasoning} leveraged LLMs to enhance semantic analysis and develop similarity metrics for domain-specialized text analysis, overcoming the limitations of traditional unsupervised NLP metrics.
